When completing and filing your tax return, there is 5 main steps to follow:
Step 1: Gather Your Documents

Make sure you have evidence for anything you are claiming on your taxes. If you were employed by an employer you should receive a T4 Statement of Remuneration Paid in order to prove how much money you made for the year through them. If you were investing, you should also receive a T5 Statement of Investment Income from the financial institution that manages your investments as evidence of how much investment income you have made for the year. You may also receive a T3: Statement of trust income allocations and designations, which most likely means you have earned investment income from a Mutual fund in a non-registered account, a personal trust or, from the estate of someone who passed away. Remember If you received Canada Emergency Response Benefit (CERB), Canada Emergency Student Benefit (CESB), Canada Recovery Benefit (CRB), Canada Recovery Sickness Benefit (CRSB), or Canada Recovery Caregiving Benefit (CRCB) payments, these are considered taxable income. You will need to file, and enter the total amount you received on your return.

Step 2: Provide and update your personal information

When filing your taxes, you will need to provide or update any personal information with the CRA. This may include information such as marital status, children in your care, banking information and your home address, the easiest way to do this is to use the CRA website through their My Account

Step 3: Report your income

Income is money you earn through employment, self-employment, and investments you have, or benefits you receive. On your return, you must report income from all sources, both inside and outside Canada. This is true even if you were paid in cash, which includes money you earn as a side job or tips you have received.
