The Canadian Revenue Agency (CRA) tries to make it as easy as possible to file your tax returns. You can always pay a tax preparer to file, however there are 4 main options that the CRA allows you to use to self-file your taxes.
Online

The CRA allows you to file your taxes online using software that they certify. A list of software products are listed at canada.ca/netfile-software. Some of the software available are free while some of them are paid. This is usually considered the easiest way to file as often times it is the quickest and if you're a novice of filing your taxes, some free software such as TurboTax Free and others have very simple ways of guiding you through the process step by step.

On paper

Filing your taxes on paper is also an option. You can see, download and order forms and publications as of January 18, 2021 at canada.ca/taxes-general-package. Starting February 5, 2021, you can call the CRA at 1-855-330-3305 to order forms and publications. If you have already filed your taxes by paper in 2019, the CRA will mail you a 2020 Income tax Package by February 19, 2021. It is important to note that due to Covid-19, there may be significant delays when processing paper return or in delivery notices of assessments and cheques through the mail. The CRA strongly recommends you to file electronically and sign up for direct deposit.

By phone

The CRA also has a phone service called File my Return. If you are eligible to use this service the CRA will mail you an invitation letter by mid February.

Community Volunteer Income Tax Program

The CRA also has a community volunteer income tax program. This is a community organization that hosts free tax clinics for Canadians with a modest income and simple tax situations. You can usually interact with these clinics by videoconference, phone, or through an arranged document drop-off. These clinics usually are offered between march and april across Canada, with some offered year-round. To found a clinic near you go to canada.ca/taxes-help.
