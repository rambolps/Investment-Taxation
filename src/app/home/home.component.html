The CRA and Canadian Taxes

The CRA (Canada Revenue Agency) is a government agency whose purpose is to ensure compliance with tax laws & policies, as well as collect taxes for the federal and most provincial and territorial governments. Most importantly, the CRA is in charge of providing tax credits and social benefit programs for Canadians in need.

One recent example of a support initiative program administered by the CRA was CERB, in response to the millions of Canadians who were involuntarily out of work due to the Covid-19 Pandemic.

Financial Glossary

tax laws & policies - regulations related to taxation and benefits

tax credits - amounts that reduce the tax you pay on your taxable income. The more tax credits that apply to you, the more you can reduce your income tax.

social benefit programs - programs to financial support social issues

CERB - Canada Emergency Response Benefit was announced on April 6 2020, to financially support employed and self-employed Canadians affected by the Covid-19 pandemic
