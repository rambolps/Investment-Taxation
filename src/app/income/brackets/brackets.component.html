What are tax brackets?

The Canadian tax system is designed in such a way so that low-income earning citizens will pay a smaller tax percentage than those who earn higher-income.

Tax brackets are categories designated to distinguish between people of varying incomes. As such, people who are in the higher-earning tax bracket will pay more tax in percentages of their income.

You must pay the federal and provincial income tax. These are the percentages you would be paying depending on which Canadian and Ontario tax bracket you fall into as a citizen of Ontario.
